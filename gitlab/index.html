Boundary

Legal logic separate from tools

Contract platforms change; the core decision trees, escalation criteria, and audit primitives should survive vendor migrations.

Scale

Async by default

Batch review, retry logic, and state transitions belong in the system — not in a spreadsheet or a person chasing status updates.

Trust

Evidence-first API shape

The answer and its basis are separate, retrievable, versioned objects. That is how legal, compliance, and security teams keep confidence in automation.

04 / AI

AI-enabled, not AI-hand-wavy.

GitLab wants AI in daily workflows. The hard part is not prompting; it is keeping AI useful, scoped, measurable, and safe enough for legal work.

CrossExamine uses a 3-stage system: deterministic triage, a 7-probe ensemble over sparse-autoencoder features, then a Claude-powered review layer with guardrails. The AI layer does not get to invent citations. Every quoted span is matched against the source document before it reaches a reviewer.

Legal Engineer transfer: the same guardrail pattern applies to contract-review AI, clause extraction, policy Q&A, self-service guidance, and customer-facing product metric explanations. AI can draft the answer; the system must verify the provenance.
